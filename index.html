Formatting Rules (AMA 11th Edition)

Authors

  • Format: LastName AB (no periods/spaces between initials)
  • Names in wrong order are corrected (e.g., Susan Doe → Doe S)
  • Rule of 3: 6+ authors truncated to first 3 + et al
  • 5 or fewer authors listed in full, no et al

Title

  • Sentence case — lowercase after colons, question marks, periods (unless proper noun)
  • Trial names kept ALL CAPS (e.g., KEYNOTE, PACIFIC)

Journal

  • Official NLM abbreviation applied
  • Journal names italicized (preserved in "Copy with italics")
  • ONLY journal names are italicized — not organizations, publishers, or websites

Published Date

  • "Published" (without "online") → deleted
  • "Published online" → deleted
  • Journal refs: year only (month/day stripped)
  • Web sources: Month Day, Year (month not abbreviated)

Volume, Issue, Pages

  • Format: volume;(issue):start-end
  • Month/day before issue number deleted
  • En dashes (–) changed to hyphens (-)
  • Identical page numbers collapsed (e.g., 100-100 → 100)
  • Abbreviated end pages expanded (e.g., 1998-02 → 1998-2002)
  • Missing page numbers flagged

Supplements

  • "Supplement" abbreviated to "suppl"
  • Formatted as (suppl N)

DOI & URLs

  • DOIs formatted as doi:10.xxxx — no https:// prefix, no trailing period
  • URLs placed at end with no trailing period
  • Missing "Accessed [date]" before URL flagged
  • Missing DOI and URL flagged

Organizational & Web Reports

  • Reports formatted as: Author(s). Title. Organization; Year. Accessed [date]. URL
  • Organization names NOT italicized

Special Formatting

  • NCCN: "NCCN. Clinical Practice Guidelines in Oncology. [cancer], version [N.year]."
  • "U.S. Food and Drug Administration" → FDA
  • Federal departments: "U.S." → "US"
  • "[package insert]" → "Prescribing information."
  • Duplicate company names: second instance removed
  • Abstract: [N] → Abstract [N] (colon removed)

Cleanup

  • PMID/PMCID information removed
  • "Abstract/Paper presented at" → "Presented at"
  • US states in conference locations abbreviated to 2-letter postal codes
  • All en dashes (–) replaced with hyphens (-) throughout
  • Duplicate references detected and noted (not removed)
This tool uses rule-based formatting (no AI). It handles the most common reference patterns well. For unusual or complex references, double-check the output.
